The 5 workflows to automate first.

Most teams try to automate everything at once and finish nothing. Here is the order I actually use with sales and ops teams — what to hand off first, why, and the trap that quietly eats the first month.

01

Lead capture & CRM enrichment

The bleed: a person retypes form fills, business cards, and inbox replies into the CRM, then googles the company to fill the blanks.

Hand off: auto-create and enrich the record the moment a lead arrives — company, size, role, source — so a human never opens a blank form again.

Trap: don't let the agent write to your CRM unsupervised on day one. Stage it, show the diff, approve, then loosen the leash.

02

Follow-up & stalled-deal surfacing

The bleed: deals die in silence because nobody remembers who went cold or what to say next.

Hand off: an agent watches the pipeline, surfaces the deal that's stuck and why, and drafts the next message. The rep reviews and sends.

Trap: automating the send before the draft is trusted. Get the drafts good first — sending is the last switch you flip.

03

Pre-call research briefs

The bleed: reps spend 15–20 minutes before each call digging through the site, LinkedIn, and past notes.

Hand off: a one-page brief auto-compiled before every meeting — context, recent activity, and the two questions worth asking.

Trap: a brief nobody reads. Put it where the rep already looks (the calendar invite or CRM), not in a new tool.

04

Quote / order / PO routing

The bleed: a request is copy-pasted between inbox, sheet, and ERP, and the pricing check happens too late.

Hand off: one tracked flow from request to approval, with the price and history check done up front and exceptions raised before they become orders.

Trap: skipping the exception path. The value is in catching the weird 5%, not in the happy path that already works.

05

Inbox triage & handoffs

The bleed: a shared inbox where everything looks urgent and replies get dropped or duplicated.

Hand off: classify, route to the right owner, and draft the reply — so people answer instead of sorting.

Trap: full auto-reply. Draft-and-approve keeps your voice and your reputation while you build trust in it.
